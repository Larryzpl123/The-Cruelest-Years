— SECTION I —

The Game in 60 Seconds

Read this page. You now know the game.

You command one of 18 European or world nations during the Second World War. Each turn, you collect resources from your cities and factories, build armies, use your leader's special ability, and move units to attack neighbours.

You win by controlling territories. There are two types of territory: cities give you gold and population; factories give you weapons. Weapons + population build new units. New units give you the chance to occupy more territory. More territory wins the war.

★ YOUR JOB ★
Hold Your Cities  ·  Capture the Enemy's
Don't Get Conquered  ·  Reach the Territory Goal

One Turn at a Time

On your turn, do these four steps in order. That's the whole game.

1
Collect
From each City: take 3 gold and 1 population.
From each Factory: take 1 weapon.
Pay unit upkeep (Fee). Can't pay? Remove the unit.
2
Produce
Spend weapons + population to build new units. Place each on any territory you control.
Newly built units are invincible this turn — no HP marker yet. They can't move or attack until next turn.
3
Abilities
Trigger your leader's active ability if you want. Pay the cost. Mark the cooldown (CD).
Passive abilities are always on.
4
Move & Attack
Move each unit up to its Speed. After moving, it may attack any enemy in Range.
A unit can attack without moving, but cannot move after attacking.

— SECTION II —

Setup

Six steps. Five minutes.
  1. Lay out the world map. 48 territories at the 9-player scale, or 38 territories at the 6-player scale.
  2. Pick a mode. Choose one of the five game modes in § III.
  3. Pick nations. Each player chooses one of the 18 nations. Starting numbers are in the Stats document.
  4. Place starting pieces. Place each nation's cities, factories, and starting units on the named territories listed in Stats § B. Take your starting gold, weapons, and population tokens.
  5. Pick your leader. Each nation has 1–3 named leaders in Stats § D. Choose one. You keep this leader for the whole game.
  6. Decide turn order. Turns go clockwise from the oldest player. Each player resolves all four steps of their turn before the next player begins.

The Speed & Range Ring

Instead of counting tiles, this edition uses a plastic ring centred on each unit. The ring is calibrated to show the unit's Speed (for movement) and Range (for attacks). The map shows each city location as a white dot with a black border. The ring tells you exactly which dots you can reach.

How to use the ring. When you move or attack, place the ring around the unit. Any white dot on the map that falls inside the ring is a legal destination (for Speed) or target (for Range). The ring removes all tile-counting. If the dot is on the line, it counts as inside.

— SECTION III —

How to Win

Hold enough territory to cross the threshold for the chosen mode, or hold the most when the campaign ends.

A territory is yours when one of your units sits on it for one full round without being driven off. If the territory has a city or factory, those become yours too — and start producing for you next turn.

Five Game Modes

There are two team configurations (Soviet Neutral, Soviet Allies) and a Free-for-All, available at two player counts (9 and 6) — five modes in total. One configuration is excluded: 6-player Team with Soviet Allies is not allowed, because at 6 players the Allies would be too strong with the Soviets on their side.

Team Mode — Axis vs. Allies

Axis: Germany, Italy, Southeastern Europe.
Allies: United Kingdom, France, Poland (and the Soviet Union if the mode is "Soviet Allies").
Neutrals (always neutral): Northern Europe, Iberian Peninsula. (Plus Soviet Union in "Soviet Neutral" modes.)

Teammates share the territory total and win together. Neutrals can win on their own by reaching the FFA threshold, or join either side mid-game. Once a neutral joins, the decision is binding.

Free-for-All

No alliances. The first player to reach the FFA territory threshold on their turn wins immediately.

Resources, Cities & Factories

Three resources. Two types of territory. That's the whole economy.

👥

🔫

The output of factories. Combined with population, weapons build new units (see unit table).

Two Types of Territory

There are two types of territory: cities give you gold and population, and factories give you weapons. A single territory may hold both a city and a factory.

Cities & Factories — Fixed Forever

You cannot build cities or factories. They start where history put them and stay there. The only way to gain one is to capture the territory it sits on.

How to Capture

  1. Move a unit onto an enemy or neutral city or factory.
  2. Stay there for one full round. If you're driven off before then, capture fails.
  3. Starting your next turn, the city or factory produces for you.
Aero units cannot capture. Planes and Bombers cannot occupy cities or factories. You need boots on the ground (or boats in port) to hold territory.

Unit Upkeep

Every unit costs gold per turn (its Fee, listed in the unit table). Pay during the Collect step. If you can't pay, the unit is removed.

— SECTION V —

Units, Movement & Combat

Everything you need to fight a battle.

Building a Unit

  1. Look up the cost in the unit table — Weapons + Population.
  2. Pay from your stockpile. Place the figure on any territory you control.
  3. Don't add an HP marker yet. A newly built unit is invincible this turn — no HP marker means no damage can be applied. The unit cannot move or attack either.
  4. Next turn, when the unit is ready to act, add the HP marker — a plastic chip or coin in your nation's colour, placed under the figure. The unit can now move, attack, and take damage.

Movement

  • Each unit can move up to its Speed, measured with the Speed & Range Ring (see § II).
  • Only one unit per territory. No stacking. (A unit may share its territory with a city or factory.)
  • A unit can attack after moving. It cannot move after attacking, unless an ability says otherwise.

Combat — Resolved on Your Turn

Combat happens during your Move & Attack step. The defender does not strike back during your turn. The defender will retaliate on their turn, if they have units left in range.

  1. Pick an enemy unit within your Range (measured by the Ring).
  2. Deal damage equal to your Attack. Remove that many HP markers from the enemy.
  3. If the enemy unit reaches 0 HP, it is destroyed and removed from the board.

Air War

  • Only Planes and Anti-Air Artillery can attack aero units. Tanks, infantry, ships — they all watch helplessly when aircraft fly overhead.
  • When a Plane attacks a ground unit, damage is reduced by 1 (ground forces can hide and disperse).
  • Bombers in this edition are short-ranged and weaker per shot than before — see the unit table. They are still excellent against unprotected ground targets, but cannot strike at long range or area-blanket as in earlier drafts.
  • Aero units cannot capture cities or factories.
All ground and naval units can hit land and sea targets. Anti-Air Artillery has the longest range in the game (4). Use it to protect industrial cities from enemy air strikes.

— SECTION VI —

Leaders & Abilities

Each nation has a leader, a national trait, and a special unit. Pick once. Keep them forever.

At setup, each player picks one leader from their nation's 1–3 named historical options (see Stats § D). The leader's ability runs for the whole game. You cannot change leaders mid-game.

Three Kinds of Ability

✶
Passive
Always on. Triggers automatically. (Example: Hitler's Total War = every 4 factories give +1 weapon.)
⚡
Active
Declare during step 3 of your turn. Pay any cost. Wait the cooldown (CD) in turns before using it again. Use a small die under each ability card to track the CD — turn the die down by 1 every turn.
⚔
Hybrid
A passive part (always on) plus an active part (declared on your turn).

Unique Unit Traits

Each nation also has a Unique Unit Trait — a passive bonus to one of its unit types. (Example: Soviet T-34 Tanks cost 1 less weapon to build.) Full list: Stats § D.

Optional: Militia

Need a cheap defender? Build a Militia instead of a regular unit. Militia cost 0 weapons and 2 population. They have 2 HP and 1 Attack but cannot move and cannot attack at distance — they're free to maintain and great at sitting on a city. Stats: Stats § C.

A Note from the Designers

Why we made this game, and what we ask you to remember.

The Second World War killed roughly seventy million people. Most of them were civilians. The Cruelest Years is a game about that war, designed by high school students for a class on game design and history.

We've made the rules simple so you can play the game in an afternoon. We hope it's fun. But fun is not the only thing we want this game to do. We also want you, while playing, to think about the choices the real leaders made — the alliances they formed, the territories they conquered, the people they sacrificed for the sake of a line on a map.

A game about WWII cannot model everything the war was. We have not put the Holocaust or the Pacific atrocities into a card. We have not given the occupied populations of Korea or Poland or France a way to suffer at the table. We made those choices on purpose: we did not want any player to perform those acts as moves. The cost of those choices is paid in our rules by the way territory is lost, and in our designer's notes by saying so plainly.

Play the game well. Take the history seriously. Read a book about the war when you put the game away.

Designed by Leon, Jeff, Oliver, and Larry.
