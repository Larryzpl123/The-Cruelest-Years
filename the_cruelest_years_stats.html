National Dossiers

Starting statistics for the eighteen playable powers. All figures are starting amounts at Turn 1.

Cities and Factories can be captured during play but never built (see Rulebook § V). Population is the starting reserve of mobilizable manpower in millions; new population is generated every turn by cities (+1 per city per turn). The note "3 (2)" in the Cities row means 3 cities total, of which 2 are colonial holdings outside the home territory.

Leaders & Unique Unit Traits

Every nation's leader cards and Unique Unit Trait. For how abilities work, see Rulebook § VIII.
How to read these cards. Each nation lists its one-to-three named leaders (choose one for the whole game), each tagged PASSIVE, ACTIVE, or HYBRID, plus a Unique Unit Trait that modifies the nation's base units. Rules of play are in the Rulebook.

Europe — Nine Powers

GermanyEurope · Axis
Adolf Hitler — Führer
Total War Passive
Every 4 Factories you control produce +1 extra weapon per turn.
Paul von Hindenburg — Reichspräsident
War Bond Hybrid
Cities produce +1 treasury (passive). When you train any unit, you may pay 4 gold to reduce its weapon cost by 1.
★ Unique Unit Trait — Tiger Tank's Blitzkrieg
Passive: All German Tanks gain +1 Attack and +1 HP, but cost +1 Weapon (so 5W / 4HP / 3A).
Active (CD 6): Pay 2 gold per chosen Tank to give those Tanks +2 Speed for one turn.
United KingdomEurope · Allies
Winston Churchill — Prime Minister
Never Surrender Active
Cost: 1 gold. When one of your military units is being attacked, flip a coin. On heads, damage is reduced by 1. May be triggered multiple times against a single attack. No cooldown.
Neville Chamberlain — Prime Minister
Appeasement Passive
If you did not attack any enemy unit this turn, gain +5 gold and +1 weapon at end of turn.
★ Unique Unit Trait — Sea Surface Minelayers
All British Boats gain +2 Attack and lose 1 HP (so 3W / 1HP / 4A — devastating but fragile).
FranceEurope · Allies
Charles de Gaulle — Free France
Free France Passive
Cities you control outside metropolitan French territory produce +3 treasury.
Édouard Daladier — Prime Minister
Rearmament Program Passive
Your Factories produce +1 weapon every 2 turns (i.e., +1 weapon on even-numbered turns).

Republican Guard Active

ItalyEurope · Axis

All Italian Mechanized Infantry gain +1 Speed and pay 1 less Fee per turn (so 2W / 1HP / 1A / Speed 4 / Fee 1).

Agendas, Policies & Plans

An optional draft deck of strategic doctrines. For how cards are played, see Rulebook § X.

Each nation will hold three Agenda cards representing its wartime strategic doctrines. The deck is optional and still in design. Each card lists a cost, an effect, a cooldown, and any precondition. Rules for playing them are in the Rulebook § X.

Worked Example · Germany's Blitzkrieg

⚡
Blitzkrieg — Germany
Cost: 1 gold per German Tank in play.
Effect: All German Tanks gain +2 Speed for one turn.
Cooldown: 6 turns.
Flavor: The lightning-war doctrine of armored breakthrough that defined the German campaigns of 1939–1941.
Card Library — In Development. The full deck of 54 cards (three per nation × eighteen nations) is still being designed and balanced. Provisional examples under consideration:
  • Soviet · Scorched Earth. Cost: 5 gold. Destroy improvements in one of your territories to deny them to an invader. Cooldown: 8 turns.
  • USA · Lend-Lease. Cost: 15 gold. Gift 10 weapons or 10 treasury to an ally. Cooldown: 4 turns.
  • Japan · Bushidō Charge. Cost: free. All Infantry +2 Attack for one turn; –1 HP next turn. Cooldown: 6 turns.
  • UK · Convoy Defense. Cost: 5 gold. Reveal one enemy naval region per turn for 3 turns. Cooldown: 5 turns.
  • China · Long March. Cost: free. Move any number of friendly units 2 tiles toward the interior without combat penalty. Cooldown: 10 turns.
